Music Sampling is a major part of music and its the process of using a portion of another individual's work and adding it into your own work.


Mainly seen in music production, music sampling can range from short snippets of a verse or beat to sometimes a remake of an entire song.


Music sampling has been a popular form of music creation since the last 1900s moving into even today as it has led to creation of many of the top songs we hear today such as:


  • Song: "Stronger" by Kanye West. Sampled: "Harder, Better, Faster, Stronger" Daft Punk
  • Song: "Ice Ice Baby" by Vanilla Ice. Sampled: "Under Pressure", Queen
  • Song: "Crazy In Love" Beyonce. Sampled: "Are you my Woman?", The Chi-Lites


Music sampling has been prominent in all genres of music but most notably Hip-Hop, Pop, R&B, and even Jazz with artists sampling music during live performances.


However, while music sampling has been such an important part of music since its creation, changing the game and allowing for new innovative ideas that have led to the creation of some of the best songs in recent history, not everyone agrees with the idea of music sampling with very notable artists such as prince stating how sampling other artists music is seen as disrespectful and unfair.


With many people and artists considering music sampling an act of plagiarism,it leaves artists who are interested in the creative side of music upset as they're not able to fully express themselves. This has led to music sampling being one of the most controversial topics as many seem to agree while others don't leading to the topic at issue, music sample clearance's .
